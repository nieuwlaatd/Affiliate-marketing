# PRODUCT.md — Harkuhh

## Register
brand — the homepage and funnel ARE the product experience; the overview/detail
pages serve the product (product register).

## Product purpose
Harkuhh is a US-market e-bike finder. It turns "I have no idea which electric
bike to buy" into a confident, personalized shortlist in under a minute. The
core mechanic is a guided funnel that starts from *where and how someone wants
to ride* and ends on a filtered, ranked list of bikes with affiliate links.
Revenue is affiliate commission, so trust and a feeling of unbiased curation
are existential.

## Users
US consumers researching their first (or next) e-bike. Mostly non-experts:
commuters tired of traffic, weekend riders, parents considering a cargo bike,
older riders wanting an easier ride. They arrive from YouTube, Pinterest and
SEO. Low patience, high skepticism of "review" sites that are thinly veiled
ad farms. They respond to clarity, honesty, and the *feeling* of the ride.

## Brand & tone
Confident, warm, a little editorial. Premium without being precious. We are a
knowledgeable friend who rides, not a salesperson. Heritage-cycling meets
modern: think a good wine label, not a SaaS dashboard. Copy is concrete and
sensory ("feel the climb disappear"), never hypey, never em-dashes.

## Strategic principles
- The funnel leads with **terrain and ride feeling**, not specs. Specs are the
  payoff, not the pitch.
- Ask only the few questions that meaningfully change the recommendation.
  Everything else is a refinement on the results page, not a gate.
- Momentum: answering should feel like riding. Single-select questions
  auto-advance; the next question is already visible, sliding in from the right.
- Every screen must feel hand-made, not category-default. An "e-bike site" does
  not have to look like every other e-bike site.

## Anti-references
- Generic green/eco e-bike sites (the category reflex).
- SaaS hero template: big gradient headline, three feature cards, CTA.
- Affiliate-farm "Top 10 Best..." listicle chrome.
- Stocky cyclist hero photo with overlaid centered text.

## Register note
Homepage + funnel = brand register (design IS the experience).
Overview, compare, detail = product register (design serves the task).
